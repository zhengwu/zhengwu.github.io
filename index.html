Short Bio:


I am currently a Postdoctoral Fellow at SAMSI Department of Statistics at The Florida State University. Before that I was an intern at the Shenzhen Key Laboratory for Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition directed by Prof. Xiaoou Tang in SIAT, Chinese Academy of Sciences. I received the B.S. degree in Engineering from South China University of Technology (SCUT) in 2008, where I was a participant of Talented Student Program.  I received a  M.S. degree in School Information Science & Technology, from  Sun Yat-Sen Unversity (SYSU) in 2010.

My main interests lie in developing techniques for functional data analysis. With the recent flurry of activities in high-throughput data, we routinely encounter complex outcomes in the form of 2D and 3D images, Euclidean curves, real valued functions, time-indexed paths on non-linear manifolds and so on. The nature of these data poses difficulties in analyzing and statistical modeling as it requires careful integration of differential geometry and statistics. First, the objects under study are infinite-dimensional. Second, these objects often lie in restricted spaces such as non-linear manifolds and Lie groups. Third, the analysis should be invariant to certain transformations. For example, in Kendall’s shape analysis, the shape of any object has to be invariant to rotation, translation and scaling by definition.

I am working with Prof. Anuj Srivastava. My current research projects include: 1) shape and functional data clustering and outlier detection (using non-parametric Bayesian method); 2) Modeling longitudinal data with non-linear features; 3) semi-parametric and/or non-parametric density estimation and comparison;
